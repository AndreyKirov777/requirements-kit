# Overview

EU Regulation 2023/1542 concerns batteries and waste batteries. It introduces the
**Digital Battery Passport** obligation and defines the data that must be recorded
and made available for different categories of battery placed on the EU market.

This is a **passive source document**. It has no managed lifecycle and does not
participate in the obligation stack — BRQ and CON reference specific articles via
`source_ref` (e.g., `SRC-COMPLY-001#article-77`).

# Key Sections Referenced

- **Article 77 — Battery passport.** Each LMT battery, industrial battery with
  capacity > 2 kWh, and EV battery placed on the market must have a battery passport
  accessible via a QR code. Defines mandatory data fields and differentiated access.
- **Annex XIII.** Information to be contained in the battery passport, and the
  parties entitled to access each category of data.
